## Priority Targets For O-1 Evidence Building (next 12 months)

1) Publishability-first lanes
- Lane A (P0): Vision-language-action agents evaluated on OSWorld + VisualWebArena + one robotics or embodied benchmark.
- Lane B (P0): Data-centric reliability for CV agents (failure taxonomies, hallucination audits, recovery loops).
- Lane C (P1): Domain benchmark extension (medical, manufacturing, mobility), with reproducible baselines.

2) Evidence categories to accumulate (O-1-oriented)
- Peer-reviewed papers (first author or major contributor) at top venues/workshops.
- Public benchmark contributions (new tasks, reproducible eval harnesses, leaderboard placements).
- Open-source artifacts with adoption (stars, forks, citations, downstream use).
- Invited talks/panels/posters at recognized conferences/labs.
- Reviewing/service record (workshop reviewer, artifact evaluator, challenge organizer).

3) Immediate next actions (2-week sprint)
- Build a 20-paper deep-read shortlist from items 1-20 + 61-78 and extract gap statements.
- Choose one benchmark triad: OSWorld + VisualWebArena + GAIA for agent reliability experiments.
- Draft a reproducible project repo template (data card, eval script, ablation template, failure taxonomy).
- Contact 8 labs from 79-90 with a one-page research pitch and replication-first proposal.
- Submit to one near-term workshop and one arXiv preprint with strong ablation + error analysis.

## Why this ranking
- P0 items maximize: (a) technical leverage, (b) citation/network visibility, and (c) measurable achievements needed for long-term O-1 evidence.
- P1 items provide high-quality adjacent depth and collaboration surface area.
- P2 items are foundational references that support stronger literature framing.
